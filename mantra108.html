Overview

Mantra108 creates community experiences around music, meditation and human connection. Kirtan is one of its central offerings — a participatory experience combining live music, mantra and shared energy.

Problem — An information-heavy event page explained Kirtan in detail but did not help first-time visitors understand what attending would feel like.

The Story

The page had all the information and none of the feeling

The original Kirtan page had grown section by section. It included the event description, benefits, Kirtan history, imagery, a testimonial and registration details. Technically, almost everything a visitor needed was there.

But the experience did not form a clear story. A first-time visitor was asked to book before understanding what Kirtan was. Benefits were hidden inside paragraphs. History appeared before the contemporary experience had been established. Images documented the community but did not explain what attending would actually feel like.

The page could describe Kirtan. It could not help someone imagine being there.

This was not simply a visual-polish problem — it was a comprehension, trust and sequencing problem. So I didn't begin by restyling individual sections. I began with the experience model: what should someone understand, feel and believe before being asked to register?

That led to one consistent journey: Promise → Benefits → Explanation → Experience → Proof → People → Community → Context → Action.

The redesign visitors see is the surface. The system underneath is what keeps the experience clear.

The System

A framework, not a page makeover

The fastest option would have been to update the hero, shorten a few paragraphs and modernise the interface. That would have improved the page visually, but the underlying problems would have remained. Instead, I established a reusable event experience system built around four layers — the Kirtan page became the first full expression of it.

01 · Colour

Colour was given a role, not just a place

Before — purple appeared across the page, but it didn't always communicate hierarchy. Pale backgrounds, buttons, decorative shapes and content sections competed without clearly defined roles.

After — the palette was organised around specific responsibilities:

Deep purple — brand & primary actions
Soft lavender — page surfaces
Pink accent — energy & movement
White — cards & profiles
Dark neutral — long-form text

The deeper purple guides action. Lavender creates breathing room. Pink carries the expressive energy of the community without overwhelming the page.

02 · Typography

A hierarchy built for scanning, then reading deeper

Before — the page relied heavily on small paragraphs and similar text treatments; headings separated sections, but the overall hierarchy didn't make the long page easy to scan.

After — a defined hierarchy lets visitors scan the headline, understand the supporting message, then read deeper when interested:

Feel Lighter
Emotional hero statement
Wait… What Even Is Kirtan?
Conversational subheading
Supporting body copy explaining the experience
Supporting body text
Event details & metadata
Practical information
03 · Spacing & Layout

A deliberate pace, not one long wall of content

Before — sections had different visual densities; long paragraphs, isolated images and large galleries created an uneven reading pace.

After — a consistent section rhythm using shared content widths, repeatable vertical spacing, defined card padding and consistent image proportions. The page now alternates between concise information, strong imagery and human proof.

04 · Imagery

Photography given a job, not just a place

Before — real moments, but images varied significantly in scale, placement and purpose. After — each image type was given a defined role:

Content as part of the system

One voice across every section

The visual inconsistency was only one part of the problem. The original experience shifted between spiritual language, formal explanation, event promotion and transactional booking copy — which could make the page harder to interpret for someone unfamiliar with Kirtan. The redesigned voice follows four principles:

Direct

Use language people understand immediately, instead of an abstract description of meditation.

"Feel Lighter"
Conversational

Use headings that reflect the questions a visitor may already be thinking.

"Wait… What Even Is Kirtan?"
Reassuring

Address uncertainty without making it feel like an objection.

"No experience needed. Come as you are."
Human

Describe the experience through feelings, people and real participation — not only spiritual terminology.

This shared voice became part of the design system because the experience wouldn't remain consistent through components alone — a reusable card with inconsistent language would still create an inconsistent product.

Component System

Every module has one job

The page was rebuilt from a collection of independent sections into a family of reusable modules — each answering a specific visitor question.

1Hero
"Why should I care?"
Creates emotional relevance through a short promise, plain-language explanation, authentic imagery and a primary action.
2Benefit cards
"What will I get from this?"
Turns long explanations into concise, scannable reasons to attend.
3Story split
"What exactly is this?"
Explains an unfamiliar idea with one strong image and a conversational heading — no academic description.
4Experience cards
"Will I feel comfortable there?"
Turns concerns into reassurance by pairing an authentic image with one clear message.
5Media feature
"What does it actually feel like?"
Gives atmosphere priority through video, since music and movement are better understood through experience.
6Testimonial system
"Did other people enjoy it?"
Builds trust through multiple attendee stories in one consistent, carousel-navigable format.
7People cards
"Who is behind this experience?"
Gives every team member equal visual treatment — portrait, name, role and a short contribution statement.
8Community callout
"Can I become more involved?"
Introduces volunteering as a separate but connected pathway from ticket registration.
9Context block
"Where does this experience come from?"
Preserves cultural and historical meaning, placed deliberately after the experience is understood.
10Event details
"When, where and how do I join?"
Consolidates schedule, location, image and registration into one predictable destination.

Responsive Behaviour

One hierarchy across every screen size

  • Multi-column cards stack into a clear reading order
  • Primary actions expand for easier mobile interaction
  • Text and imagery rearrange without changing the narrative sequence
  • Consistent image cropping across viewport sizes
  • Reduced decorative elements where they could compete with content
  • Testimonial and benefit groups adapt to swipeable or stacked formats
  • Event details stay grouped rather than breaking into disconnected fragments

The desktop page uses width to create visual energy. The mobile version uses sequence and spacing to preserve clarity.

Before & After
Before

Sections behaved differently from each other. Benefits lived inside paragraphs. Photography had no consistent role. Calls to action appeared without a clear confidence-building sequence. Historical, emotional and practical information competed within the same hierarchy.

After

Every section belongs to the same family. Colour communicates hierarchy. Typography creates a consistent reading pattern. Components have defined responsibilities. Calls to action follow moments of understanding and trust.

Outcome

The redesign created more than a refreshed Kirtan page — it established a reusable foundation for how Mantra108 can introduce experiences online.

One visual hierarchy across colour, type and imagery
One conversational voice across every section
One modular component family, ready to reuse
One responsive framework for future event pages

Visitors can now understand what Kirtan is, see what attending looks like, meet the community and find practical information — without navigating a dense or fragmented experience. The redesigned experience created a clearer path from first-time discovery to event registration, and established a reusable framework for future Mantra108 event pages.

"The system doesn't make every event page look identical. It makes every event feel recognisably Mantra108."
